Australian Mammals Overview

Common Name Scientific Name Habitat Activity Rhythm Social Structure Diet
Northern Yellow-Bellied Glider Petaurus australis Arboreal, inhabiting eucalyptus forests. Nocturnal, primarily active at night. Lives in lifelong pairs or family groups. Omnivore, feeding on nectar, fruits, and insects.
Lumholtz’ Tree-kangaroo Dendrolagus lumholzi Arboreal, residing in rainforests. Cathemeral, active during the day. Solitary, except during mating season. Folivore, primarily consuming leaves and fruits.
Long-tailed Pygmy Possum Cercartetus caudatus Arboreal, found in heathlands and woodlands. Nocturnal, foraging at night. Solitary, with little social interaction. Omnivore, feeding on nectar, insects, and fruits.
Striped Possum Dactylopsida trivirgata Arboreal, inhabiting tropical rainforests. Nocturnal, primarily active at night. Solitary, with a strong territorial behavior. Insectivore, eating fruits, insects, and small vertebrates.
Rat-kangaroo Hypsiprymnodon moschatus Terrestrial, found in dense undergrowth. Nocturnal, foraging at night. Solitary, with occasional social interactions. Omnivore, primarily feeding on roots and tubers.
Red-legged Pademelon Thylogale stigmatica Terrestrial, inhabiting forests and grasslands. Crepuscular, active during dawn and dusk. Lives in mobs, providing safety in numbers. Herbivore, primarily grazing on grasses and leaves.
Northern Brown Bandicoot Isoodon macrourus Terrestrial, found in forests and scrublands. Nocturnal Solitary, with overlapping territories. Omnivore, feeding on insects, worms, and plant material.
Mareeba Rock Wallaby Petrogale mareeba Terrestrial, inhabiting rocky outcrops and woodlands. Crepuscular Lives in small groups Herbivore, primarily grazing on grasses and leaves.
Common Brushtail Possum Trichosurus vulpecula Arboreal, found in urban and forested areas. Nocturnal, primarily active at night. Solitary, but can be found in loose groups. Omnivore, feeding on leaves, fruits, and insects.
Eastern Gray Kangaroo Macropus giganteus Terrestrial, inhabiting open grasslands and forests. Crepuscular, active during dawn and dusk. Lives in mobs, providing safety in numbers. Grazer, primarily feeding on grasses.
Atherton Antechinus Antechinus godmani Terrestrial, found in rainforests. Nocturnal, foraging at night. Solitary, with males dying after breeding season. Carnivore, primarily feeding on insects and small invertebrates.
Northern Quoll Dasyurus hallucatus Terrestrial, inhabiting forests and rocky areas. Nocturnal, primarily active at night. Solitary, with overlapping territories. Carnivore, feeding on insects, small mammals, and reptiles.
